# Granite Carpentry Cape Town Expansion Strategy

Congrats on your success in Gauteng! Expanding to Cape Town is a smart move. Here's my enhanced advice to make your expansion smooth and profitable:

## 1. Pre-Launch Priorities (Next 2-4 Weeks)

### Find Your Cape Town Team Lead First
Before anything else, you need **one trusted person on the ground** in Cape Town:
- **Project Manager/Site Supervisor** who can:
  - Meet clients for site inspections
  - Oversee installation quality
  - Handle urgent issues
  - Coordinate multiple installer teams
  
**Where to find them:**
- Poach from competitors (offer better pay/flexibility)
- Construction management groups on LinkedIn
- Ask your Gauteng suppliers if they know reliable CT contacts

### Secure Your First 2-3 Installers
Start small with quality over quantity:
- **Trial period approach**: 1-2 test jobs before committing
- Look for installers who:
  - Have own transport (critical in CT's spread-out geography)
  - Send regular photo updates
  - Have liability insurance
  - Work with a consistent helper/team

**Red flags to avoid:**
- No references or portfolio
- Can't commit to timelines
- Poor communication response times

Since you're staying in Joburg, automate what you can:

### Digital Job Management
**Use a simple system like:**

- **Daily Photo Protocol:**
  - Before starting (empty space)

### Payment Structure for Installers
Protect yourself while being fair:
- **50% deposit** when materials arrive on site
- **50% on completion** after you receive final photos + client confirmation
- Weekly payments (builds loyalty)

## 5. Launch Strategy (First 90 Days)

### Month 1: Soft Launch
- **5-10 jobs** in your priority areas (Camps Bay, Claremont, Green Point)
- Offer **10-15% launch discount** to build portfolio
- Focus on: Getting great photos, testimonials, and referrals

### Month 2: Optimize
- Review what worked/didn't work
- Identify your best 2-3 installers
- Refine pricing based on CT travel times and material costs
- Start building relationships with interior designers/architects

### Month 3: Scale
- Increase to 15-20 jobs/month
- Add 1-2 more installer teams if demand is there
- Launch targeted Facebook/Instagram ads for Cape Town
- Start requesting Google reviews from CT clients

## 6. Financial Considerations

### Pricing Adjustments for Cape Town
- **Material costs**: May differ slightly from Gauteng (check supplier pricing)
- **Travel time**: Factor in CT traffic (N2, M3 can be brutal)
- **Coastal considerations**: Salt air = different sealant/finishing products needed

### Cash Flow Management
- Keep **separate bank account or tracking** for CT operations
- Budget for:
  - Emergency trips to CT (flights/accommodation)
  - Initial marketing spend (R10,000-R15,000)
  - Material deposits with new suppliers
  - Insurance coverage extension to Western Cape

## 7. Risk Mitigation

### Protect Your Business
- **Contracts with installers**: IP protection, non-compete (reasonable radius), quality standards
- **Client contracts**: Clear scope, timelines, payment terms, warranty details
- **Insurance**: Public liability, professional indemnity covering Western Cape operations
